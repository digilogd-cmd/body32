## 1. Brand foundation

### 1.1 Brand name

**BODY32**

Always write the master brand in uppercase in logos, product headers, and formal references. Sentence-case usage is allowed only when required by accessibility or platform conventions.

### 1.2 Primary tagline

> Decode Your Body. Balance Your Life.

Korean working translation:

> 몸의 신호를 이해하고, 삶의 균형을 찾아보세요.

The Korean line is a localized expression, not a literal word-for-word translation. Final campaign copy may vary, but the meaning must remain centered on discovery and balance.

### 1.3 Category

K-Wellness Personality Platform

For users unfamiliar with the category, use:

> A wellness personality experience inspired by Eastern philosophy.

### 1.4 Brand idea

BODY32 gives people a memorable language for discussing personal patterns of energy, routine, recovery, and balance.

### 1.5 Brand promise

BODY32 offers a beautiful and approachable way to discover, understand, and express a wellness personality—without diagnosing or judging the user.

## 2. Positioning

### 2.1 Positioning statement

For globally curious adults who enjoy identity and wellness experiences, BODY32 is a character-led self-discovery platform that transforms a short reflective quiz into one of 32 distinctive Guardians and a personal Passport. Unlike clinical assessments, supplement funnels, or generic personality quizzes, BODY32 combines transparent lifestyle reflection, premium visual craft, and Korean-inspired cultural storytelling.

### 2.2 What makes BODY32 distinctive

- A stable system of 32 identities rather than disposable quiz results
- Guardian characters designed as long-term brand IP
- A Passport users can keep, share, and revisit
- Korean and Eastern-philosophy inspiration presented for a global audience
- Wellness language that avoids diagnosis, shame, and fear
- A product foundation that can expand from a web experience into daily app use

### 2.3 Competitive boundaries

BODY32 must not visually or verbally present itself as:

- A hospital, clinic, laboratory, or traditional medicine practice
- A supplement store or affiliate-sales funnel
- An astrological prediction service
- A scientifically validated personality assessment
- A childish mascot quiz
- A copy of an existing wellness, productivity, or consumer technology brand

External products may be used as quality references only. Their layouts, illustration styles, iconography, distinctive motion, or trade dress must not be replicated.

## 3. Brand personality

### 3.1 Personality spectrum

| BODY32 is | BODY32 is not |
| --- | --- |
| Calm | Passive |
| Insightful | Authoritative |
| Warm | Childish |
| Premium | Exclusive |
| Contemporary | Trend-dependent |
| Culturally rooted | Stereotypical |
| Optimistic | Unrealistically positive |
| Mysterious in small doses | Obscure or superstitious |
| Clear | Clinical |

### 3.2 Emotional sequence

The first session should intentionally move through five emotions:

1. **Curiosity** — “What is my BODY32 type?”
2. **Safety** — “This feels respectful and easy.”
3. **Anticipation** — “My result is being assembled.”
4. **Recognition** — “I can see myself in this interpretation.”
5. **Pride** — “I want to keep or share this Passport.”

## 4. Voice and tone

### 4.1 Core voice

BODY32 speaks like a calm, culturally informed guide. It observes patterns, explains unfamiliar concepts simply, and gives the user room to interpret the result.

### 4.2 Voice rules

- Lead with the user's experience, not the system's intelligence.
- Describe tendencies rather than fixed traits.
- Use concise sentences and concrete language.
- Explain why a question is optional when it requests personal information.
- Avoid inflated claims such as “perfect,” “scientifically proven,” or “AI diagnosis.”
- Never rank one type as healthier, stronger, more evolved, or more desirable.
- Use encouragement without prescribing behavior.

### 4.3 Contextual tone

| Context | Tone | Example direction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Landing | Curious and confident | Introduce the 32-type idea in one breath |
| Profile | Respectful and transparent | Explain optional data and privacy |
| Quiz | Conversational and neutral | Ask one clear question at a time |
| Analysis | Anticipatory, not deceptive | Say “assembling your result,” not “AI diagnosing” |
| Result | Warm and insightful | Lead with strengths and balanced reflection |
| Recommendation | Gentle and practical | Offer options, not treatment instructions |
| Error | Calm and actionable | State what happened and how to continue |
| Privacy | Direct and plain | Avoid marketing language |

### 4.4 Language patterns

Prefer:

- “Your answers suggest…”
- “You may feel most balanced when…”
- “A pattern worth noticing…”
- “Try this as a small reflection.”
- “You can skip this question.”

Avoid:

- “Your body is broken or imbalanced.”
- “This type always suffers from…”
- “Your blood type proves…”
- “Take this supplement to correct…”
- “AI detected a health problem.”

## 5. Naming system

### 5.1 Master brand and product features

Use stable English feature names across locales when they function as branded artifacts:

- BODY32
- BODY32 Guardian
- BODY32 Passport
- BODY32 Balance
- BODY32 Compare

Localized explanatory labels may appear alongside branded terms.

### 5.2 Type identity

Every type will have:

- A stable machine ID, such as `TYPE_01`
- A stable public code determined in TASK-002/003
- A localized Guardian name
- A localized descriptive title
- An element and energy classification

Do not use real celebrity names as official type names. Any future similarity content involving public figures requires rights, legal, cultural, and reputational review.

## 6. Logo direction

### 6.1 Required lockups

Future logo exploration must produce:

- Primary horizontal wordmark
- Compact symbol plus wordmark
- Symbol-only app icon
- Monochrome light and dark variants
- Small-size favicon variant
- Passport emboss or stamp variant

### 6.2 Concept principles

Potential concepts may explore:

- The number 32 as a modular identity system
- Balanced opposing forms
- A subtle portal, orbit, fingerprint, or elemental cycle
- A “B” and “32” relationship without sacrificing legibility

Avoid:

- Medical crosses, DNA helixes, hospital shields, or ECG lines
- Yin-yang symbols used as decoration without cultural purpose
- Literal human body silhouettes
- Generic leaf logos
- Complex five-element diagrams at favicon size
- Gradient-heavy marks that fail in monochrome

### 6.3 Clear space and minimum size

Exact measurements will be finalized with the selected mark. Until then:

- Reserve clear space equal to at least the cap height of the “B.”
- Do not place the wordmark below 88 CSS pixels wide in digital UI.
- Use the compact symbol for smaller placements.
- Never stretch, outline, rotate, recolor selectively, or add effects to the master mark.

## 7. Visual identity

### 7.1 Visual principles

1. **Warm space:** generous breathing room and warm neutral surfaces
2. **Quiet structure:** clear alignment and hierarchy without visible complexity
3. **Elemental focus:** color appears with purpose, especially around Guardians and results
4. **Soft depth:** elevation supports interaction instead of decoration
5. **Measured motion:** transitions guide attention and respect reduced-motion settings
6. **Premium character:** Guardians feel collectible and crafted, not toy-like or generic

### 7.2 Color roles

The interface uses a restrained semantic palette. Guardian-specific palettes may add expression inside result art, Passport backgrounds, and data visualization, but they must not redefine control states or accessibility semantics.

Primary visual direction:

- Warm off-white background
- Charcoal text
- Forest action color
- Clay warmth
- Navy depth
- Gold used sparingly for premium detail

Exact roles and values are defined in `docs/DESIGN_SYSTEM.md`.

### 7.3 Photography

Photography is optional for the MVP. If used:

- Show real, diverse people in calm everyday settings.
- Prefer natural light, authentic posture, and environmental space.
- Avoid medical examinations, anatomy overlays, before/after bodies, idealized fitness imagery, and stereotypical “Eastern” props.
- Do not mix stock photography heavily with Guardian illustration on the same screen.

### 7.4 Graphic motifs

Approved motif families may include:

- Soft orbital paths
- Modular 32-cell grids
- Abstract elemental fields
- Passport stamps and coordinate marks
- Fine balance lines

Motifs should support hierarchy and storytelling. They must not become decorative noise or imply scientific measurement.

## 8. Guardian art direction boundaries

TASK-002 will define the complete system. The brand-level requirements are:

- All 32 Guardians belong to one visual universe.
- Each must remain recognizable at avatar size.
- Silhouettes, emblems, and palettes must be distinct.
- Expression should be confident and warm rather than infantile.
- Avoid direct imitation of named commercial illustration systems.
- Avoid culturally sacred or sensitive symbols without review.
- Production art requires a documented prompt/process, human art direction, and consistency QA.
- Every image needs an accessible text description and non-image type label.

## 9. Passport brand rules

The Passport is a collectible result artifact, not identification or certification.

It must:

- Display BODY32 prominently
- Feature the user's Guardian and stable type code
- Use user-controlled display information only
- Remain legible at social-story and mobile-card sizes
- Avoid government emblems, nationality claims, or official-document language
- Include a subtle wellness/entertainment context when shared outside the product

## 10. Brand safety and approval checklist

Before approving public-facing brand material, confirm:

- No medical or diagnostic implication
- No unsupported scientific claim
- No type hierarchy or body shaming
- No hidden commercial recommendation
- No copied brand asset or distinctive trade dress
- No unexplained cultural symbol
- Korean and English meaning remain aligned
- Guardian usage follows the Character Bible
- Contrast and readability meet the Design System
- User data shown in shared assets is explicitly controlled by the user
